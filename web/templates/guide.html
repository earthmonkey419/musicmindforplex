Everything you need to know to dig into your stacks — from setup to advanced filtering.

MusicMind for Plex is a self-hosted AI music companion for your Plex library — natural language playlists, deep listening analysis, and a smarter genre system, all running on your own hardware.

🚀 Quick Start

1
Install dependencies: sudo python3.12 -m pip install plexapi openai flask mutagen essentia numpy --break-system-packages (essentia powers Synapse and voice/instrumental analysis — heavier to install, but required)
2
Get your Plex token — play any item in Plex, click ... → Get Info → View XML, and copy the X-Plex-Token value from the URL.
3
Copy config.example.py to config.py and fill in your Plex URL, token, OpenAI key, and (optionally) your Last.fm key and a free AcoustID key (enables fingerprinting and Various Artists resolution).
4
Run the initial setup scripts in order — ingest your library, AI-tag your tracks, enrich artist metadata, and sync Last.fm if configured. See the README for the full sequence.
5
Start the app: sudo pm2 start python3.12 --name "musicmind" -- ~/musicmind/web/app.py then open http://YOUR_NAS_IP:8787 in your browser.
Tip: After setup, use ⚙️ Back Office → Full Sync any time you add new music — it scans Plex, ingests, syncs Last.fm, enriches artists, and tags tracks in one click.

🎵 Generating Playlists

Type anything into the prompt box and hit Preview. MusicMind figures out what you mean:

Mood or vibe

"late night psychedelic soul", "grandma's birthday party" — expands into 2-3 focused vibes and searches your library's AI tags. Phrases like "comfort picks" or "old favorites" automatically bias toward tracks you actually play a lot; "popular tracks" or "iconic songs" bias toward tracks that are widely well-known (a real, measured signal pulled from Plex, not a guess).

Title search

"songs with ocean in the name" — searches track titles directly, no AI needed.

Artist search

"tracks by Miles Davis" — finds tracks by that specific artist.

Filter queries

"female Brazilian artists from the 1970s" — detects gender, country, and era, applies them as filters automatically.

📻 Last.fm date search

Type lastfm: played in december 2023 (or any date range in plain English) to pull tracks straight from your real listening history — no genre matching, just what you actually played. Or use the On This Day date picker below the Preview button for a quick, AI-free way to see what you were listening to around a specific date (an 8-day window centered on your pick). If any of your real scrobbles from that window never matched a local track, they show up separately below the results, clearly labeled — never added to the playlist, just so you can see what's missing from your library.

Genre focus

11 genre checkboxes above the prompt box (Jazz, Hip-Hop/Rap, Classic/Garage/Alt/Punk Rock, and so on) narrow the AI to that genre's own tag vocabulary — leave all unchecked to search your full tag library. Checking one or more automatically switches to strict mode: the AI still reads and interprets your whole prompt, but only ever picks tags from the genres you selected, so "something for a rainy day" + Jazz genuinely returns jazz, not just "rainy day" music that happens to touch on jazz occasionally.

Filters

  • Unplayed only — restrict to 0-play tracks
  • Instrumental only — restrict to tracks with no (or minimal) vocals. Once checked, a vocal tolerance slider appears (default 0.55) — drag it down for stricter instrumental-only results, or up to allow tracks with some quiet or background vocal presence. Grays out automatically if voice/instrumental analysis hasn't been run yet on your library, with an explanation why.
  • Genre — Decades (search artist era) and Genres (search AI tags), shown as two sections in the dropdown
  • From year / To year — restrict by release year range
  • Tracks per artist — cap how many tracks from one artist can appear (default 1)
  • Min BPM / Max BPM — restrict by real, measured tempo (requires Synapse analysis — see below)
  • Danceability — restrict by Synapse's measured danceability score
  • Min rating — restrict by your Plex star rating
  • Gender / Country / Era — filter by artist metadata
Tip: Prompt and filters combine. "Late night jazz" + Gender=female + Era=60s finds late night jazz from female artists of the 60s.

🎯 Based On

Pick a track or an artist, get similar results — two genuinely different modes under one box:

🎵 Based on a track

"Based on: Up On the Catwalk" finds tracks that share the most AI tags with it, refined by how close their real, measured tempo and key are (via Synapse). Entirely local, measured data — no AI involved in the actual matching. The seed track itself is included as the anchor, and results are automatically sequenced into a smooth tempo arc.

🎤 Based on an artist

"Based on: Chris Isaak" finds tracks by genuinely similar musicians, using Last.fm's real listening-pattern data (who other people actually listen to alongside this artist) — not a guess. Falls back to asking AI directly only if Last.fm has nothing for that specific artist. The artist's own top tracks are included alongside the similar artists' tracks, and results are sequenced for tempo flow.

Start typing a song or artist name into the box — suggestions from your library appear as you type, clearly labeled by type. Pick one to see the results.

⚙️ Back Office

One-click maintenance jobs. Each streams live output so you can watch progress.

  • Full Sync — the complete pipeline: scan → ingest → Last.fm → MusicBrainz enrich → AI fallback enrich → tag tracks
  • Scan / Sync Plex Library — pull new or changed tracks
  • Sync Last.fm — update scrobbles, play counts, loved tracks
  • Tag New Tracks — AI genre tagging
  • Refresh Context Playlists — rebuild Your Afternoon, Weekend Flow, Often Together
  • Enrich Artists — MusicBrainz and AI Fallback (see below)
  • Refresh Gap Analysis — re-run Missing From Your Collection
Tip: Long-running jobs may time out behind a reverse proxy or tunnel. Use your NAS's direct IP (http://YOUR_NAS_IP:8787/admin) for anything that takes a while.

🧠 Synapse

Synapse analyzes the actual audio in your library — not metadata, not AI guesses — measuring tempo (BPM), musical key, and danceability directly from the waveform using Essentia, and (if you've downloaded the voice/instrumental model — see the repo README) also verifies each track's real instrumental status the same way, in the same pass. Completely free — no API calls, no OpenAI cost — and runs entirely on your own hardware.

This is real, measured data, and it powers real features already shipped: the Min BPM / Max BPM and Danceability filters on the main page, the vocal tolerance slider, Based On track-mode similarity, and the per-track breakdown on Playlist Audit.

The coverage bar at the top of this page shows how much of your library has been analyzed so far. The first full-library run is the only slow one — it can take a couple of days depending on your library size and hardware, and is safe to run in the background. After that initial backlog is cleared, Synapse runs automatically as the final step of every Full Sync, analyzing only genuinely new tracks — typically seconds to minutes, not days.

📊 Count Audio File Types

Walks your library and shows a format breakdown (MP3, FLAC, etc). Read-only, fast.

⏱️ Estimate Time

Analyzes a few real sample tracks live, on your hardware, to project how long a full run would take. No permanent writes.

🔬 Limited Run

Runs the real analysis but capped to a small number of tracks — a safe way to confirm everything works before committing to the full multi-day job.

🚀 Full Run

Analyzes every remaining track and stores the results — the one-time backlog clear, or a manual re-run on demand. Resumable, safe to stop and restart anytime, and runs fully detached from the app itself, so it keeps going even if the app restarts.

The Errors panel shows tracks that couldn't be analyzed — corrupted files, unreadable formats, or files that timed out — clearly labeled by whether it was the tempo/key analysis or the voice/instrumental check that failed. Safe to ignore; these are skipped automatically on future runs.

Tip: Run Estimate Time first, then a small Limited Run to confirm everything works, before starting the full job — every NAS performs differently, and it gives you a real number for your specific hardware before you commit to a multi-day background job.

🎼 MusicBrainz & AI Enrichment

Every artist gets enriched with gender, country, and era metadata using two layers:

🔍 MusicBrainz (free, first)

Looks up real, structured data — gender and country — from the free MusicBrainz database. No cost, no API key needed.

🤖 AI Fallback (OpenAI)

Fills in era for every artist (a judgment call MusicBrainz can't reliably answer), plus gender/country for any artist MusicBrainz couldn't confidently match.

Genre tags (the AI subgenre tagging used by the playlist generator) are separate and stay entirely in MusicMind's own database — Plex is not touched unless you explicitly run the optional genre write-back script.
